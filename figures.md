<p align="center">
Fig 3: Topographical Changes Before and After the Construction of the Flood Control Structure in Ketenisse, Belgium
</p>
<div align="justify">
Elevation maps showing three different views of a study area (a) 22-09-2021 (b) 29-01-2024 (c) 08-05-2024 and (d) and (e) shows the elevation change from 22-09-2021 to 29-01-2024 and 29-01-2024 to 08-05-2024 respectively. Before the flood structure the western region had always the higher elevation (max 6.08 mTAW) (Fig a and b). However, the eastern part has dropped over time (min 3.61 mTAW and 3.49 mTAW) (Fig a and b) which is in flood risk zone. It occurred due to erosion from 2021 to 2024 (max 0.62 mTAW) in both eastern and western region. After making the flood structure the elevation becomes higher (min 3.70 mTAW) (Fig c). This results sedimentation in the affected region (max 0.58 mTAW). 
</div>

<div align="justify">
Slope maps showing the gradient of the terrain in a study area across three different dates: (a) 22-09-2021, (b) 29-01-2024, (c) 08-05-2024. In the image of 2021 average slope has been found below 9.56° (Fig a). However, over time, the slope increased which shows a lot of red colors (53°-84°) to the eastern region due to erosion in 2024 (Fig b). After construction of the structure slope has been reduced below 9.56° again (Fig c).
